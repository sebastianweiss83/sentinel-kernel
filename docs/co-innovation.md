# Co-Innovation Partners

Sentinel is shaped by organisations deploying AI in regulated, sovereign,
and classified environments. Partners are not named publicly. What matters
is the stakeholder type and the requirements they bring.

---

## Partner categories

### Innovation drivers

Organisations building AI-enabled hardware and autonomous systems for
regulated markets. They have strong capability at the sensor, data, and
mission layers but need a sovereign decision record layer to unlock
government and defence procurement. Proprietary US-hosted governance tools
are a structural non-starter for their customer base.

**What they shape:** Trace schema completeness, real-time performance
requirements, air-gapped deployment patterns.

### Sovereign operators

Government-adjacent IT infrastructure organisations responsible for deploying
technology within classified or sovereign networks. Their driver: a certified
middleware layer they can operate on their own infrastructure without dependency
on external vendors — and which serves as the deployment channel into broader
government systems.

**What they shape:** BSI IT-Grundschutz mapping, VS-NfD prerequisites,
storage backend requirements for classified environments.

### Research and validation partners

Security research institutions and academic bodies that validate Sentinel's
security posture, contribute to formal verification of the trace integrity
model, and provide independent assessment for certification processes.

**What they shape:** Security audit methodology, formal verification
requirements, publication-ready compliance evidence.

### Governance and open source bodies

Organisations involved in open source governance, foundation stewardship,
and standard-setting. Their input ensures Sentinel's governance model is
credible to regulators, procurement committees, and the contributor community.

**What they shape:** RFC process, foundation governance structure,
licence and trademark policy.
